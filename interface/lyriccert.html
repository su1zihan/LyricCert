Process

How it works.

Three stages from raw text to a structured content rating.

01
Preprocessing
Input lyrics are lowercased, contraction-expanded, tokenized and WordNet-lemmatized. Non-alphabetic tokens are removed before encoding.
02
Longformer Inference
A fine-tuned Longformer processes up to 2,048 tokens. The CLS token receives global attention; mean pooling feeds a linear classification head with four sigmoid outputs.
03
Rating Assignment
Raw probabilities are passed through calibrated thresholds to assign one of five MCR tiers (M-E to M-AO) and generate content descriptors.
Rating System

The rating system.

The Music Content Rating (MCR) system maps model outputs to five audience tiers, modelled on existing media classification frameworks.

M-E
Everyone
No significant content detected across all four categories.
M-P
Parental Guidance
Minor thematic content present. Suitable for most audiences.
M-T
Teen Audiences
Moderate scores in one or more categories. Teen audiences and above.
M-R
Restricted
High scores in one or more categories. Not recommended for minors.
M-AO
Adults Only
Explicit content detected across multiple categories. Adults only.
